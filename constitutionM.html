Constitution

ARTICLE 1: NAME

The name of the organization shall be the Engineering Society of LSC-Montgomery.

ARTICLE 2: OBJECTIVE

The purpose of this organization shall be as follows: To create a suitable environment for students to: grow technical knowledge and abilities, enhance ability to work as a team members, and develop leadership qualities of its members. To define a suitable project where members can apply their knowledge and technical skills each academic year (September to May). Such project shall result in at least one physical working engineering model. It should be based on multidisciplinary knowledge. To promote real-world understanding of current problems in multiple fields of knowledge, by inviting guest speakers to share their industrial and/or business experience with the members of the organization also by organizing field/industrial visits. To assist its members with networking opportunities to facilitate their professional development.

ARTICLE 3: MEMBERSHIP

SECTION 1

Any registered student (full time or part time) at LSC-Montgomery who is in good standing with the college and fulfills the membership requirements which coincide with the purpose of the organization is eligible for membership. Alumni, faculty and staff of LSC-Montgomery may participate as members but shall not have voting privileges or serve as officers of the organization.

Section 2:

Privileges of active student members include participation in community and campus activities organized by the Engineering Society, voting, nominations, serving as an officer, meeting attendance, and fundraising efforts.

Section 3:

To guarantee equal rights to all members of the LSC-Montgomery student body, equal opportunities shall be offered to all students without regard to race, color, national/and or ethnic origin, religion, creed, gender, marital status, sexual orientation, age, citizenship, veteran status, or physical ability.

ARTICLE 4: THE ELECTION OF OFFICERS

SECTION 1: OFFICERS OF ENGINEERING SOCIETY

The officers of the Engineering Society shall consist of a President, Vice President, Secretary and Treasurer. They shall be elected by ballot vote at the annual election meeting, and these officers, together with the faculty advisor, shall constitute the Executive Board with the authority to transact the business of the Engineering Society between the meetings.

SECTION 2: ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

To be eligible for an officer position (an “office”), a member must be in good standing with the Engineering Society. The member must be enrolled as a student at LSC-Montgomery. The member must maintain a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 at the beginning of the fall semester, which must be maintained to hold office. One person can run for two offices but can only accept one. No one person shall hold more than one office.

SECTION 3: RESIGNATION OF OFFICERS

Any officer desiring to resign shall submit a written letter of resignation to the Executive Board explaining the reasons for the action. The approved resignation shall become effective after the successor has been elected. An officer leaving LSC-Montgomery permanently during his/her term of office shall notify the Society Advisor in addition to a letter of resignation.

SECTION 4: VACANCIES IN THE EXECUTIVE BOARD

Officers shall serve for one year (two semesters); however, if their office becomes vacant they shall be replaced by re-election, except in the case of the president. If the office of the president is vacant, the Vice President will carry out the President responsibilities until the next annual election meeting, and a new officer will be elected to the position of Vice President.

SECTION 5: ELECTIONS

All student members may nominate and vote in an election. All the active members present will have right to vote and their vote will stay anonymous. The timeline for election of Executive Committee officers will be: each spring semester for the following academic year. In case of vacant office, there will be special elections during the academic year.

ARTICLE 5: MEETINGS

SECTION 1: GENERAL MEETINGS

Members should meet at least twice a month over the academic year. Officers should announce the businesses conducted between the meetings in these general meetings.

SECTION 2: EXECUTIVE BOARD MEETINGS

The Executive Board will meet a minimum of once every two weeks during an academic year.

SECTION 3: NOMINATION MEETING

In the spring semester meetings, a nominating committee will be set-up to receive nominations for the office of the following year. This committee, headed by the Vice President, shall be responsible for producing the list of nominees of the offices of the Executive Board to all member of the Engineering Society.

SECTION 4: ELECTION MEETINGS

The annual election meeting shall be in the end of the spring semester of each year, at which the new officers shall be elected. All members should be present.
